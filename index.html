Welcome

Welcome to this space dedicated to the M2D2 Talks co-organized by Valence Discovery and Mila - Quebec AI Institute.

From applied research papers to open source projects, weâ€™re hoping to use these talks to help demystify AI for drug discovery and make the field more accessible for newcomers. M2D2 will bring our vibrant AI & drug discovery communities together and spark new perspectives, provoke discussions, and offer a safe space to share new ideas.

A wide range of drug discovery related topics will be covered reflecting the vibrant diversity of tools and methodologies in the community:

  • Applications of machine learning in computational molecular design
  • Representation learning for small molecules and macromolecules
  • Prediction of molecular properties and bioactivities
  • Machine learning for quantum chemistry and molecular dynamics
  • Generative models for de novo molecular design
  • Active learning & uncertainty quantification
  • Multiparameter optimization and compound selection
  • Interpretable and explainable activity models
  • Reaction prediction, retrosynthesis, and synthesis planning

The Next Talk

Challenges of Therapeutics Machine Learning in the Wild

Date: Wed, Jan 19, 2022 @ 10:00 AM EDT Speaker: Kexin Huang
Bio: Kexin Huang is a CS PhD student at Stanford, advised by Prof. Jure Leskovec. His research interests lie in algorithmic challenges arising from real-world machine learning and biomedicine, with a focus on graph learning and therapeutics. He has conducted ML research in various companies and institutions such as Pfizer, IQVIA, Flatiron Health, Dana Farber Cancer Institute, and Rockefeller University.
Abstract: Machine learning for therapeutics offer incredible opportunities for expansion, innovation, and impact. Despite promises, many challenges exist. In this talk, the speaker will first highlight two high-impact but relatively understudied directions - ML-aided clinical trial design and low-data/cross-context biomedicine. Then, he will discuss challenges arising from therapeutics ML adoption in the wild, namely, generating actionable hypotheses and user interface with domain scientists. Lastly, challenges in infrastructure, such as data and benchmark, will be looked at.
